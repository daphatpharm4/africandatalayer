### 2. Positioning

Section headline:
Most decisions about African markets are made without ground truth.

Body copy:
Organizations rely on aggregated datasets, outdated reports, static listings, and assumptions. But markets move faster than data. Streets change. Informal businesses relocate. New commercial clusters emerge. Distribution gaps appear block by block.

That gap is where capital is misallocated, operations underperform, and high-growth neighborhoods are missed.

Key message:
The risk is not lack of analysis. The risk is acting on stale or incomplete reality.

### 3. Problem

Section headline:
What teams assume is often not what is happening on the ground.

Body copy:
Market expansion, strategy, and investment teams often work from datasets that are too old, too broad, or too detached from real field conditions.

Across African cities:

- Informal economies shift rapidly
- Commercial activity evolves block by block
- Business openings and closures happen faster than traditional reporting cycles
- Infrastructure and service availability vary by micro-zone
- Traditional datasets fail to capture real conditions

The result:

- Failed expansions
- Inefficient distribution
- Missed high-growth zones
- Poor route and coverage planning
- Weak market entry assumptions
- Higher execution risk in informal and dynamic economies

### 4. Insight

Section headline:
Africa does not have a data problem. It has a ground-truth problem.

Body copy:
Many organizations already have reports, dashboards, forecasts, and spreadsheets. The missing layer is not another abstract dataset. The missing layer is continuously verified neighborhood intelligence that reflects real-world conditions.

Existing data is often:

- Too aggregated
- Too infrequent
- Too detached from field reality
- Too slow for operational decisions
- Too weak on informal and fast-changing activity

What is missing:

- Verified field observations
- Geolocated evidence
- Repeated updates
- Neighborhood-level granularity
- Confidence signals that separate observed reality from assumptions

### 5. Solution

Section headline:
African Data Layer is a living intelligence layer built from the ground up.

Body copy:
ADL combines field-based data collection networks, geospatial mapping systems, and continuous verification cycles to create a dynamic, decision-grade data layer.

The platform captures, verifies, structures, and updates real-world observations so clients can understand what is changing across neighborhoods, corridors, and commercial clusters.

How it works:

- Field agents capture geolocated observations on mobile devices
- Submissions include GPS, photo evidence, timestamps, and structured attributes
- Verification workflows check accuracy, duplication, metadata, and trust signals
- Cleaned records become structured datasets and map layers
- Repeat collection cycles turn static points into living market intelligence

Outcome:
Teams get a data layer that reflects real-world conditions in near real time, not a frozen snapshot of what the market used to look like.

## Sample Dataset Requirements

The sample dataset should prove the product without exposing sensitive data.

Recommended columns:

- `point_id`
- `category`
- `country`
- `city`
- `neighborhood`
- `latitude`
- `longitude`
- `observation_date`
- `last_verified_at`
- `verification_status`
- `confidence_score`
- `source_type`
- `primary_attributes`
- `change_status`
- `notes_redacted`

Recommended sample views:

- Map layer with verified points
- Category filter
- Confidence filter
- Neighborhood density summary
- Change-over-time panel
- Export preview

Privacy and safety requirements:

- Remove contributor identifiers
- Remove raw photos unless cleared for public use
- Generalize sensitive coordinates if needed
- Redact business owner names and phone numbers
- Include only approved categories and markets
- Add watermark if used publicly
