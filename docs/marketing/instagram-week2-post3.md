## Concept

Launch Fun Fact Friday as a savable, share-forward weekly format. Pick one surprising fact, teach it clearly, tie it back to why ADL ground-truth mapping matters. Post #01 topic: **Cameroon's "pharmacie de garde" rotating night-duty system** — a real OTC pharmacy access mechanism most non-Cameroonians have never heard of, and a textbook example of data that goes stale weekly without field verification.

**Why carousel:** Swipeable reveals work for fact-drop content — hook, setup, fact, "wait what," payoff, CTA. Six slides lets the payoff breathe and maximizes save signal. IG re-serves carousels to non-swipers, stretching reach past a Friday one-shot.

**Why this fact:** Non-controversial, culturally rooted, educational for foreign audience, nostalgic for Cameroonian audience, maps directly onto ADL pharmacy vertical value prop (opening hours + garde rotation = volatile data → perfect ground-truth use case).

## Caption (EN primary)

> Fun Fact Friday #01 — In Cameroon, the pharmacy open tonight is not the same as last Friday.
>
> It's the "pharmacie de garde" system: licensed pharmacies in every quartier rotate 24/7 duty on a weekly schedule set by the regional pharmacists' council. One neighborhood, one open pharmacy, all night — then next week the baton passes to the next shop on the list.
>
> It's elegant. It's Cameroonian. And it's nearly invisible on Google Maps.
>
> Static directories can't keep up with a schedule that shifts weekly. Families end up calling around at 2am. That's exactly the kind of gap ground-truth field data is built to close.
>
> Every Friday we'll drop one fact from the field. Follow so you don't miss #02.
>
> Save this if you learned something. Share with a friend who didn't know about garde.

- Pharmacie de garde is a documented public Cameroonian healthcare infrastructure — referenced by MINSANTE and the Ordre National des Pharmaciens du Cameroun (ONPC). Safe to describe without sourcing a specific figure.
- No accuracy percentages claimed. No submission counts. No internal thresholds.

- No real agent names, photos, or identifying data.
- No competitor named as under-performing — "static directories" and "Google Maps" referenced factually, not disparagingly.
- No medical advice. No drug names. No prescription guidance.
