## Concept

Pre-tagged from FFF #02's story question harvest: fuel price volatility in Cameroon. Most people outside the country assume pump prices are static national rates. Most people inside know they aren't — they shift by station, by week, by region, by black-market arbitrage when official supply falters. ADL's fuel-station vertical exists precisely because static directories cannot hold this. Six slides build the cultural truth, name why it matters for residents and analysts, and route back to the verified-data promise.

**Why this fact:** Reader-relatable in-country, eye-opening abroad, and zero controversy when framed observationally. Connects directly to ADL's fuel-station vertical (price + supply state captured at the pump). Pre-tagged in FFF #02 roadmap.

**Why carousel:** Same payoff structure as FFF #01 and #02 — hook, setup, why, why-it-matters, why-static-maps-miss-it, CTA. Six slides. Locks the FFF visual identity (gold series stamp, terra underline, navy backbone) for the third week in a row — series equity compounds.

**Claim posture:** Strictly qualitative. No prices, no percentages, no shortage frequencies. "Often", "in many quartiers", "from week to week" — observational language only, per content rules.
