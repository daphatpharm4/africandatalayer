## Concept

Pre-tagged from FFF #03's roadmap. Static maps treat transport routes as fixed lines: a road exists, a bus stop sits at a corner, a taxi rank lives at point X. Anyone in Douala knows that's a fiction. Moto-taxi pickup spots shift with traffic, taxi-brousse routes adjust to fuel and security, informal stops appear at lunch and disappear by dusk. Six slides build the cultural truth and route back to the verified-data promise.

**Why this fact:** Reader-relatable in-country, eye-opening abroad. Connects to the transport vertical without naming any operator. Pre-tagged in FFF #03 roadmap.

**Why carousel:** Same payoff structure as FFF #01–#03 — hook, setup, why, why-it-matters, why-static-maps-miss-it, CTA. Six slides preserve series visual identity (gold series stamp, terra underline, navy backbone).

**Claim posture:** Strictly qualitative. No counts, no shares of mode, no operator names. "Often", "in many quartiers", "from morning to evening" — observational only.
