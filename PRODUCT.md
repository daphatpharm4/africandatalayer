# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

adaptive

## Users

African Data Layer serves three connected audiences, with field contributors as the primary product surface.

- Field agents capture and enrich real-world infrastructure data outdoors, often one-handed on mid-range Android phones, in bright sunlight, with intermittent connectivity and limited battery.
- Admin reviewers triage submissions, detect fraud, coach contributors, and protect data quality through fast, high-confidence review workflows.
- Clients and data consumers inspect trustworthy changes, monitor coverage, and export presentation-ready outputs.

The primary job is to make the next useful contribution obvious and quick while preserving offline continuity, verified quality, and contributor trust.

## Product Purpose

African Data Layer turns distributed field observations into trustworthy, usable infrastructure intelligence. It combines map-native capture, evidence and fraud checks, review operations, progress feedback, tenant-configurable projects, and decision-ready outputs across web, iOS, and Android.

Success means contributors can complete high-value work reliably in real field conditions, reviewers can establish confidence quickly, and clients can understand provenance and deltas without manual reconciliation.

## Positioning

Turns distributed field observations into trustworthy, usable infrastructure intelligence.

## Brand Personality

Credible, immediate, motivating.

The product should feel operational, map-native, and contribution-forward. It should convey confidence, momentum, and clarity. Progress and reward cues should encourage verified contributions without making serious field operations feel like an arcade game.

Reference qualities:

- Uber: decisive hierarchy and task momentum.
- Google Maps: spatial clarity, familiar interaction, and grounded utility.

## Anti-references

- Neon, crypto-style, or glossy dark-dashboard aesthetics.
- Decorative game effects that compete with field tasks.
- Low-contrast premium minimalism that fails outdoors.
- Dense desktop controls forced onto narrow mobile screens.
- Novel navigation patterns where familiar map and operational patterns are clearer.

## Design Principles

1. Operational before ornamental. Every design choice must improve clarity, motivation, or trust.
2. Momentum makes contribution easier. Always expose the next high-value action and make verified progress feel immediate.
3. Quality beats volume. Hierarchy, rewards, and feedback should reinforce verified value rather than raw activity.
4. Map-native familiarity wins. Prefer spatial and navigation patterns that feel inevitable to Uber and Google Maps users.
5. Field reality sets the bar. Bright sunlight, one-handed use, intermittent connectivity, bilingual content, and limited battery are default conditions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA across web surfaces. Maintain at least 48px touch targets for primary mobile actions, strong daylight contrast, keyboard and screen-reader support, English/French parity, safe text wrapping, color-independent status cues, and complete reduced-motion fallbacks. Core field workflows must remain usable with unreliable connectivity and on mid-range devices.
